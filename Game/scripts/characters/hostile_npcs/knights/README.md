# Knight Scripts

Knight scripts are a separate hostile NPC family, not an extension of the Banshee story controller.

- `KnightController.gd` owns shared movement, collision, health, attack scaffolding, respawn hooks, encounter dialogue hooks, and the simple melee/ranged attack branch.
- `KnightTuning.gd` is the editable per-variant stat resource. Melee and ranged knights should keep distinct tuning resources; ranged behavior is enabled by assigning a projectile scene.
- Non-lethal damage enters a short hurt/stun state from tuning, cancels active attack hitboxes, and then resumes chase, return-home, or default behavior. Lethal damage goes straight to death.
- Ranged projectile launching requires both `KnightTuning.projectile_scene` and a scene-authored `ProjectileSpawn` marker. Melee knights should leave both absent.
- `KnightHealth.gd`, `KnightHealthBar.gd`, and `KnightArrowProjectile.gd` are local support scripts for this hostile family.

Future levels should use a level flow controller plus the shared `KnightCampEncounterController` when knight/campfire state needs to be saved.

For camp knights, the placed scene instance is the roster entry. Its editor-authored position becomes its home and respawn point, and `campfire_base_path` links it to the campfire whose destruction disables respawns for the current level visit. Camp variants should use one active roster slot per tent. Use the `default_behavior` export for pre-aggro idle, pacing, or conversation setup; camp-wide aggro and return-home coordination belongs in `KnightCampEncounterController`.

Knight body collision preserves the scene-authored `MovementBox.disabled` state. The current Basic Knight scenes are authored with live body collision enabled, using the `HostileNPC` layer and colliding with World, Player, and NPC bodies, but not other HostileNPC bodies. Use compact circular movement footprints, floating/top-down `CharacterBody2D` motion, a small safe margin, `wall_min_slide_angle = 0`, and the shared top-down movement helper with real physics `delta` so knights do not snag on static tile seams. Dead or hidden defeated knights still disable their body collision until respawn restores the authored alive setting. `NavigationAgent2D` supplies path points around camp/world navigation polygons and should not use actor avoidance or `velocity_computed` movement while avoidance is disabled. First-detection barks are short-lived and coordinated by the camp helper so several knights do not stack unreadable bubbles at once. Camp knights use navigation path points for aggro and return-home movement; do not add direct-chase fallback, launch windows, or side-step detours to knight movement. Melee knights may use small stable navigation target offsets outside attack range to avoid perfect overlap, then drop those offsets once close enough to attack. Melee attack entry uses tuning `attack_range` plus a tiny contact tolerance, not large helper-area extents. After aggro clears, return-home should temporarily tighten navigation completion distances if needed, continue until the knight reaches its authored home position, settle exactly there, restore authored chase navigation distances, and only then resume default idle, pacing, or conversation.

Detection barks do not pause aggro. The camp helper should force linked live knights into chase as soon as the owning campfire base detects the player and starts the bark event. When camp aggro clears, knights enter return-home and keep moving until they reach normal arrival distance, then settle exactly on the authored home position before resuming default behavior.
